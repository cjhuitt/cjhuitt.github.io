Reading Review, 2023

 2023.12.30  2023.12.31  Review  316  2 mins

Annual reading statistics and observations at the end of 2023…

Most of this info taken from The Story Graph.

Overall

I started 68 books and finished 65. 2 were abandoned with no plans to revisit, and I’m currently reading 1 with little chance to finish before midnight.

I also read 2 magazines not yet included in the totals (I haven’t added the

This is 5 (8%) more books than last year, which wasn’t intentional but not unuusal given variations in book length, etc.

Composition

Most of my reading was Science Fiction and Fantasy – 60 books total. 32 (50%) were fantasy and 28 (43%) Science Fiction.

I read 4 nonfiction books last year. I’d like to improve that in the coming year.

Timing

I had a small slump in reading around May and June, and then a bigger slump in October and November. I’m not sure what was happened in the earlier slump, but the latter was lead-up and immersion in NaNoWriMo, when I intentionally use a lot of my traditional reading time to try and write instead.

Format

As in past years, the vast majority of my reading was electronic. I think I read 1 book in phsyical format.

Selection

As mentioned above, only 4 books (6%) were nonfiction, and I want to read more of those.

For a simple look at some diversity metrics, 36 books (55%) were by female (or presenting) authors; 23 (35%) male (or presenting); and 7 (11%) were collections, collaborations, or not easily identified.

The Superlatives

I read 3 books I gave maximum ratings to: - Jade City and Jade Legacy by Fonda Lee - Bookshops & Bonedust by Travis Baldtree

The best series I read/finished should come as no surprise given that, which was The Green Bone Saga by Fonda Lee.

My most-read authors were Tanya Huff (9 books), Brandon Sanderson (6 books), and Seanan McGuire/Mira Grant (5 books)
